**Effective change:** vendor write actions (`endpoint.isolate`, `network.blocklist_add`, `policy.deploy_device_config`, `patch.deploy_special_devices`, `ticket.create_remediation`) execute by default at Gate 1 without waiting for human approval — the mandatory HITLGate block is removed from the happy path. Compensating controls stay fully intact and are now the primary safety net: kill switch (KS-L1–L4, <5 s propagation), governance-kernel budget/effect/DLP gates, least-privilege scoped action credentials, hash-chained audit, blast-radius tiering. HITL is retained only as an **anomaly-escalation path** (low-confidence verdicts, sandbox TIMEOUT/OOM per D-9, extreme-blast-radius outliers) — not as the default approval gate for normal writes.

Files updated same pass: `kill-switch-hitl.md` (§7 contract rewritten, H5 TBD resolved), `governance-kernel.md` (HITLGate row), `safety-overview.md` (§2 "never unattended" headline claim), `confidence-calibration.md` (§2 cross-ref), `mcp-security.md` (§1 corrected-note), `catalogs.md` (agent catalog, feature-flag catalog, vendor-action catalog), `traceability-matrix.md` (US-004/016/018 gate labels), `product-overview.md` (out-of-scope list), `mitigation-write-path.md` / `security-stepper.md` / `chat-guidance.md` (feature copy).

**Risk carried forward, on the record:** this removes the human-approval step for actions that isolate endpoints, modify network blocklists, deploy patches, and open tickets on customer production infrastructure, based solely on AI-agent judgment, before any operating track record existed at the maturity bar the original Gate-3 fence was written to require. The credential-honeypot threat model (`safety-overview.md` §2b) and STRIDE/OWASP defense-in-depth layers (L1–L8) are unchanged in design and now carry more of the safety burden than before, since the human check they used to backstop is gone from the default path.
