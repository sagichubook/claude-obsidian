# Sagi

## Overview

Founder of [[Dux, Inc.]]. Sole named decision-maker across the entire `docs/` corpus — every judgment call that isn't a mechanical/factual correction is recorded in [[Dux Decisions Log]] as "Sagi's call," from D-1 (2026-07-05) through D-57 (2026-07-21). Talpiot-alumni founder; the vision-reference corpus also carries an Israel Security Prize / Prime Minister's Prize detail (per two independent corroborating sources).

## Key Facts

- Authored the corpus's authority hierarchy and its central editorial rule: specs state current truth in the present tense; all change history lives in decisions-log.md only (D-12).
- Personally narrowed the claims-alignment directive (D-10) after an earlier version of it had gone far enough to justify an unattended-write safety posture by quoting marketing copy — a self-correction recorded openly rather than erased.
- Drove the two largest architecture pivots: the full self-hosted-Kubernetes stack replacement (D-33, 2026-07-19) and its partial reversal to EKS + Bedrock direct-SDK + Agentic RAG (D-34, same day), each on stated commercial/technical rationale (portability for finance/healthcare buyers; FedRAMP/GovCloud capability).
- Works the open-items register (`OI-##`) personally, item by item, explicitly declining to make Founder/Legal/GTM-owned calls unilaterally through engineering-only remediation passes (e.g., leaving OI-09/32/26/27 open across multiple 2026-07-16 sessions until he closed them himself).
- $9M seed round closed December 2025 with a three-way co-lead — unrelated to the "seed playbook" operational-maturity terminology used elsewhere in the corpus (see [[Dux Overview]] stage-model note).
